# Pullback and Pushout

Pullbacks and pushouts are limits and colimits over diagrams of shape $\bullet \to \bullet \leftarrow \bullet$ and $\bullet \leftarrow \bullet \to \bullet$, respectively. They generalize fiber products, intersections, and amalgamated sums, and play a central role in algebraic geometry (fiber products of schemes) and homological algebra (connecting exact sequences).

---

## Pullback (Fiber Product)

<Definition title="Pullback" number="2.7">
Given morphisms $f : A \to C$ and $g : B \to C$ in a category $\mathcal{C}$, a **pullback** (or **fiber product**) is an object $A \times_C B$ together with morphisms $p_A : A \times_C B \to A$ and $p_B : A \times_C B \to B$ such that $f \circ p_A = g \circ p_B$, and for every object $X$ with morphisms $\alpha : X \to A$, $\beta : X \to B$ satisfying $f \circ \alpha = g \circ \beta$, there exists a unique morphism $h : X \to A \times_C B$ with $p_A \circ h = \alpha$ and $p_B \circ h = \beta$.

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
A \times_C B & \xrightarrow{p_B} & B \\[6pt]
\downarrow\scriptstyle{p_A} & & \downarrow\scriptstyle{g} \\[6pt]
A & \xrightarrow{f} & C
\end{array}
$$
</Definition>

---

## Pushout (Amalgamated Sum)

<Definition title="Pushout" number="2.8">
Given morphisms $f : C \to A$ and $g : C \to B$, a **pushout** (or **amalgamated sum**, or **fibered coproduct**) is an object $A \sqcup_C B$ with morphisms $\iota_A : A \to A \sqcup_C B$ and $\iota_B : B \to A \sqcup_C B$ such that $\iota_A \circ f = \iota_B \circ g$, and satisfying the dual universal property.

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
C & \xrightarrow{g} & B \\[6pt]
\downarrow\scriptstyle{f} & & \downarrow\scriptstyle{\iota_B} \\[6pt]
A & \xrightarrow{\iota_A} & A \sqcup_C B
\end{array}
$$
</Definition>

<Remark title="Duality">
A pushout in $\mathcal{C}$ is a pullback in $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}$. Every result about pullbacks has a dual statement about pushouts.
</Remark>

## Examples

<Example title="Pullback in Set" id="pb-set">
In $\mathbf{Set}$, the pullback of $f : A \to C$ and $g : B \to C$ is

$$A \times_C B = \{(a, b) \in A \times B : f(a) = g(b)\}$$

with projections to $A$ and $B$. When $f$ and $g$ are inclusion maps of subsets $A, B \subseteq C$, the pullback is the intersection $A \cap B$.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback as preimage" id="pb-preimage">
In $\mathbf{Set}$, the pullback of $f : A \to B$ along the inclusion $\{b\} \hookrightarrow B$ is the fiber $f^{-1}(b)$. More generally, pulling back along any map gives the "preimage" construction.
</Example>

<Example title="Fiber product of schemes" id="pb-scheme">
In $\mathbf{Sch}$, the pullback $X \times_S Y$ is the fiber product. For affine schemes, $\mathrm{Spec}\, A \times_{\mathrm{Spec}\, R} \mathrm{Spec}\, B = \mathrm{Spec}(A \otimes_R B)$. This is the fundamental construction in algebraic geometry: base change, fibers, intersections, and self-products are all fiber products.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback in Grp" id="pb-grp">
In $\mathbf{Grp}$, the pullback of $f : G \to K$ and $g : H \to K$ is $G \times_K H = \{(g,h) \in G \times H : f(g) = g(h)\}$ with the induced group structure. When $f$ and $g$ are inclusion maps of subgroups, this recovers the intersection.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback in Top" id="pb-top">
In $\mathbf{Top}$, the pullback of $f : X \to Z$ and $g : Y \to Z$ is the set $\{(x,y) : f(x) = g(y)\} \subseteq X \times Y$ with the subspace topology from the product topology on $X \times Y$. The fiber $f^{-1}(z)$ is the pullback along the inclusion $\{z\} \hookrightarrow Z$.
</Example>

<Example title="Pushout in Set" id="po-set">
In $\mathbf{Set}$, the pushout of $f : C \to A$ and $g : C \to B$ is the quotient $(A \sqcup B) / {\sim}$ where $f(c) \sim g(c)$ for all $c \in C$. This "glues" $A$ and $B$ along the common part specified by $C$.
</Example>

<Example title="Pushout in Ab" id="po-ab">
In $\mathbf{Ab}$, the pushout of $f : C \to A$ and $g : C \to B$ is $(A \oplus B) / \{(f(c), -g(c)) : c \in C\}$. This is the **amalgamated sum** or **fiber sum**.
</Example>

<Example title="Pushout in Top: CW complexes" id="po-cw">
CW complexes are built by pushouts. Attaching an $n$-cell to a space $X$ via a map $\varphi : S^{n-1} \to X$ is the pushout of $\varphi$ and the inclusion $S^{n-1} \hookrightarrow D^n$:

$$X \cup_\varphi D^n = X \sqcup_{S^{n-1}} D^n$$

The entire CW structure is a sequence of pushouts.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback and kernel" id="pb-kernel">
In an abelian category, the kernel of $f : A \to B$ is the pullback of $f$ and the zero map $0 \to B$:

$$\ker f = A \times_B 0$$

Dually, the cokernel is a pushout.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback of covering spaces" id="pb-covering">
In $\mathbf{Top}$, if $p : \tilde{X} \to X$ is a covering space and $f : Y \to X$ is any continuous map, the pullback $f^*\tilde{X} = Y \times_X \tilde{X}$ is a covering space of $Y$. This is the "pullback" or "induced" covering space.
</Example>

<Example title="Pushout in Ring" id="po-ring">
In $\mathbf{CRing}$, the pushout of $R \to A$ and $R \to B$ is the tensor product $A \otimes_R B$. This is because the pushout in $\mathbf{CRing}$ is the coproduct in the category of $R$-algebras.
</Example>

<Example title="Pullback preserves monomorphisms" id="pb-mono">
If $g : B \to C$ is a monomorphism, then the pullback $p_A : A \times_C B \to A$ is also a monomorphism. This is because pullbacks "preserve" monomorphisms — a general property of limits.
</Example>

## Properties

<Theorem title="Pullback of monomorphisms" number="2.3">
In any category, if the square

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
P & \to & B \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow\scriptstyle{g} \\
A & \xrightarrow{f} & C
\end{array}
$$

is a pullback and $g$ is a monomorphism, then $P \to A$ is also a monomorphism.
</Theorem>

<Theorem title="Pasting law for pullbacks" number="2.4">
Given a commutative diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccccc}
P & \to & Q & \to & R \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow & & \downarrow \\
A & \to & B & \to & C
\end{array}
$$

if the right square is a pullback, then the outer rectangle is a pullback if and only if the left square is a pullback.
</Theorem>

<Remark title="Looking ahead">
Pullbacks and pushouts are key to the construction of [exact sequences](/category-theory/ch03-abelian-categories/concepts/c04-exact-sequence) in abelian categories and the [Snake Lemma](/category-theory/ch03-abelian-categories/theorems/t01-snake-lemma). In the theory of triangulated categories, the mapping cone triangle generalizes the pushout construction.
</Remark>
